Adoption FAQs

What are the adoption fees?

Adoption fees vary depending on the pet and the shelter/rescue organization. Typically, fees range from $50-$300 and include spay/neuter, vaccinations, microchipping, and sometimes a starter kit of food and supplies.

What if the pet doesn't work out in my home?

Most shelters and rescues have a return policy if the adoption doesn't work out. We encourage adopters to be patient during the adjustment period (typically 2-4 weeks), but if it's truly not a good fit, you can return the pet to the organization you adopted from.

Can I adopt if I live in an apartment?

Yes! Many pets are well-suited for apartment living. When searching, look for pets described as "apartment-friendly" or consider smaller breeds, senior pets, or cats that are typically more adaptable to smaller spaces.

How long does the adoption process take?

The timeline varies but typically takes 3-7 days from application to bringing your pet home. Some shelters offer same-day adoptions for pre-approved applicants, while others may have a more thorough screening process.
